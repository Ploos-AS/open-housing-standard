# 8. Governance

**Normative**

---

# 8.1 Purpose

This section defines the governance model for the Open Housing Standard (OHS).

The purpose of the governance model is to ensure that OHS remains open, technically consistent, vendor-neutral, and developed through transparent consensus.

---

# 8.2 Openness

All normative discussions SHALL be conducted in public.

Meeting minutes, design discussions, and accepted decisions SHOULD be publicly available whenever practical.

---

# 8.3 Participation

Any individual or organization MAY propose improvements to an OHS specification.

Participation SHALL NOT require membership fees or commercial affiliation.

---

# 8.4 Proposals

Normative changes SHALL be proposed through the project's documented contribution process.

Each proposal SHALL:

- describe the problem;
- describe the proposed solution;
- explain compatibility implications;
- identify affected standards.

---

# 8.5 Architectural Decisions

Changes affecting the architecture of OHS SHALL be documented as Architecture Decision Records (ADRs).

Accepted ADRs become part of the permanent project history.

---

# 8.6 Consensus

Normative changes SHOULD be accepted through rough consensus.

When consensus cannot be reached, project maintainers SHALL make the final decision based upon:

- technical merit;
- consistency with OHS principles;
- long-term maintainability;
- interoperability.

---

# 8.7 Transparency

All accepted normative changes SHALL be traceable.

The following SHALL be publicly available:

- change history;
- issue discussions;
- ADRs;
- released specifications.

---

# 8.8 Backward Compatibility

Published standards SHOULD remain backward compatible whenever practical.

Breaking normative changes SHALL require a new major version.

---

# 8.9 Editorial Changes

Editorial improvements MAY be accepted without changing normative behavior.

Editorial corrections SHALL NOT change conformance requirements.

---

# 8.10 Conflicts of Interest

Participants SHOULD disclose conflicts of interest when proposing normative changes.

Technical decisions SHALL prioritize the objectives of OHS rather than commercial interests.

---

# 8.11 Intellectual Property

Contributions SHALL be compatible with the licensing model of the Open Housing Standard project.

Normative specifications SHALL remain freely available.

---

# 8.12 Repository

The official Git repository SHALL constitute the authoritative source for all OHS specifications.

Published releases define normative versions.

---

# 8.13 Appeals

Any participant MAY request reconsideration of an accepted decision.

Appeals SHALL include technical justification.

Project maintainers SHALL review appeals in a transparent manner.

---

# 8.14 Governance Evolution

The governance model MAY evolve over time.

Changes to this section SHALL themselves follow the governance process defined herein.
