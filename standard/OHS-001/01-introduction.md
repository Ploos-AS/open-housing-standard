# 1. Introduction

## 1.1 Purpose

The Open Housing Standard (OHS) defines an open, vendor-neutral
framework for designing homes that are durable, accessible, repairable,
and technology-friendly.

The purpose of OHS is to establish common architectural and technical
principles that improve long-term quality, reduce lifecycle costs, and
encourage interoperability between building systems and digital tools.

OHS serves as a reference for architects, engineers, builders, software
developers, educators, homeowners, and organizations seeking an open
approach to residential design.

## 1.2 Vision

> **To create homes that remain practical, maintainable, and desirable
> for generations.**

## 1.3 Mission

The mission of OHS is to establish an open residential specification,
promote durability, universal accessibility, repairability, local-first
digital infrastructure, reduced complexity, and an open ecosystem.

## 1.4 Guiding Philosophy

Residential buildings should be designed as long-lived infrastructure
rather than disposable consumer products.

## 1.5 Goals

1.  Define a vendor-neutral housing standard.
2.  Improve long-term housing quality.
3.  Increase accessibility and adaptability.
4.  Simplify maintenance and upgrades.
5.  Encourage open digital documentation.
6.  Support interoperable BIM workflows.
7.  Publish reference house designs.
8.  Enable validation and automation tools.

## 1.6 Non-Goals

OHS does not replace building regulations, structural engineering,
professional architectural judgement, or product certification.

## 1.7 Design Lifetime

Reference designs should target a service life of **100 years or more**.

## 1.8 Audience

Architects, engineers, builders, municipalities, educators, software
developers, homeowners, and open-source contributors.

## 1.9 Relationship to Other Standards

OHS complements existing standards and regulations rather than replacing
them.
