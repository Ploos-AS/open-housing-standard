# 2. Scope

## 2.1 Purpose of this Specification

This specification defines the overall framework of the Open Housing
Standard (OHS). It establishes the principles, terminology, governance
model, and document architecture for the OHS specification series.

## 2.2 In Scope

OHS covers long-term durability, universal accessibility, repairability,
vendor-neutral design, technology-ready infrastructure, digital
documentation, reference house designs, and interoperable software
tooling.

## 2.3 Out of Scope

OHS does not replace building regulations, structural engineering,
professional architectural judgement, prescribe architectural style,
certify construction quality, or approve manufacturers.

Compliance with OHS alone does not imply legal approval to construct a
building.

## 2.4 Relationship to Building Regulations

Applicable legislation always takes precedence over OHS where conflicts
exist.

## 2.5 Intended Implementations

Architects, engineers, builders, municipalities, educators, software
developers, homeowners, and open-source communities.

## 2.6 Reference Houses

Reference houses demonstrate implementations of OHS specifications and
are informative unless explicitly stated otherwise.

## 2.7 Digital Assets

CAD, BIM, IFC, schemas, documentation, software, and automation scripts
support interoperability.

## 2.8 Jurisdictional Neutrality

OHS is designed to be internationally applicable.

## 2.9 Evolution of the Standard

The OHS document series is intended to evolve while preserving a stable
architecture.
