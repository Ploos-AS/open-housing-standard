# 2. Scope

## 2.1 Purpose of this Specification

This specification defines the overall framework of the Open Housing Standard (OHS).

It establishes the principles, terminology, governance model, and document architecture that form the foundation of the OHS specification series.

This document does not define detailed architectural, technical, or construction requirements. Such requirements are defined by the individual OHS specifications referenced by this document.

---

## 2.2 In Scope

The Open Housing Standard covers the specification of residential buildings with an emphasis on:

- long-term durability;
- universal accessibility;
- repairability and maintainability;
- open and vendor-neutral design;
- technology-ready infrastructure;
- digital documentation;
- reference house designs;
- interoperable software tooling.

OHS specifies *what* should be achieved rather than prescribing specific commercial products or construction methods.

---

## 2.3 Out of Scope

The Open Housing Standard does not:

- replace national or local building regulations;
- replace structural engineering;
- replace professional architectural judgment;
- prescribe aesthetic or architectural style;
- certify construction quality;
- approve products or manufacturers;
- define legal compliance within any jurisdiction.

Compliance with OHS alone does not imply legal approval to construct a building.

---

## 2.4 Relationship to Building Regulations

OHS is intended to complement existing building regulations rather than replace them.

National, regional, and local regulations remain authoritative within their respective jurisdictions.

Where conflicts exist between OHS and applicable legislation, the applicable legislation takes precedence.

---

## 2.5 Intended Implementations

The standard is intended for implementation by:

- architects;
- engineers;
- residential builders;
- municipalities;
- educational institutions;
- software developers;
- homeowners;
- open-source communities.

The standard may also be used as the basis for reference implementations, educational material, procurement requirements, and software validation.

---

## 2.6 Reference Houses

OHS defines a series of official reference houses.

Reference houses demonstrate practical implementations of one or more OHS specifications.

Reference houses are informative implementations and should not be interpreted as normative requirements unless explicitly stated.

---

## 2.7 Digital Assets

The OHS project includes digital assets supporting implementation of the standard.

These assets may include:

- CAD models;
- BIM models;
- IFC files;
- documentation;
- configuration files;
- validation schemas;
- software tools;
- automation scripts.

Such assets are intended to improve interoperability and reproducibility.

---

## 2.8 Jurisdictional Neutrality

OHS is designed as an internationally applicable standard.

The specification intentionally avoids assumptions tied to any specific country, legal framework, climate, language, or construction industry.

Regional adaptations may be published separately without modifying the core OHS specifications.

---

## 2.9 Evolution of the Standard

The Open Housing Standard is intended to evolve over time.

New specifications, reference implementations, and supporting tools may be added without changing the overall architecture established by OHS-001.

Backward compatibility should be preserved whenever practical.
