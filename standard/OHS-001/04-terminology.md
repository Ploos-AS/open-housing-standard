# 4. Terminology

This section defines the terminology used throughout the Open Housing Standard (OHS).

The key words **MUST**, **MUST NOT**, **REQUIRED**, **SHALL**, **SHALL NOT**, **SHOULD**, **SHOULD NOT**, **RECOMMENDED**, **MAY**, and **OPTIONAL** in all OHS standards are to be interpreted as described in RFC 2119 and RFC 8174.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, the following definitions apply.

---

## 4.1 Building

A permanent structure intended for human occupation.

Within OHS, the term *building* refers to the complete physical structure including all integrated systems.

---

## 4.2 Dwelling

A self-contained residential unit intended for one household.

A dwelling may exist as a detached house, semi-detached house, row house, apartment, or similar residential unit.

---

## 4.3 House

A detached residential dwelling.

Reference houses published by OHS represent detached houses unless otherwise specified.

---

## 4.4 Reference House

A complete example implementation demonstrating conformance with one or more OHS standards.

Reference houses are informative unless explicitly declared normative.

---

## 4.5 Standard

A normative specification published by the Open Housing Standard project.

Standards define requirements that implementations may claim conformance to.

---

## 4.6 Requirement

A normative statement that specifies mandatory or optional behaviour.

Requirements are expressed using the terminology defined in RFC 2119 and RFC 8174.

---

## 4.7 Conformance

The state in which an implementation satisfies every mandatory requirement of the referenced OHS standard.

Conformance applies only to published versions of a standard.

---

## 4.8 Reference Implementation

An implementation produced by the OHS project for verification and demonstration purposes.

Reference implementations assist understanding but do not replace the normative specification.

---

## 4.9 Service Life

The intended period during which a building is expected to perform its required functions with normal maintenance.

Unless otherwise specified, OHS assumes a design service life of at least **100 years**.

---

## 4.10 Accessibility

The degree to which a dwelling can be safely and independently used by people with varying physical, sensory, or cognitive abilities.

Accessibility requirements are defined by applicable OHS standards.

---

## 4.11 Adaptability

The ability of a dwelling to accommodate future changes in occupancy, technology, or user needs with limited structural intervention.

---

## 4.12 Maintainability

The ease with which building components may be inspected, repaired, replaced, or upgraded during the service life of the building.

---

## 4.13 Technical Core

The collection of building services and infrastructure including, but not limited to:

- water
- wastewater
- electrical distribution
- ventilation
- heating
- communications
- automation

The Technical Core provides a structured and maintainable location for building services.

---

## 4.14 Wet Zone

An area containing plumbing fixtures or requiring waterproof construction.

Examples include bathrooms, laundry rooms, and utility rooms.

---

## 4.15 Dry Zone

An area that does not normally contain plumbing fixtures requiring waterproof construction.

---

## 4.16 Open Format

A documented format that is publicly available and implementable without vendor restrictions.

Whenever practical, OHS standards SHOULD use open formats.

---

## 4.17 Open Standard

A specification that is publicly available and may be implemented by any party without discriminatory licensing restrictions.

---

## 4.18 Vendor Neutral

A requirement that does not depend on products or technologies from a single manufacturer.

---

## 4.19 Smart Home Ready

A building designed such that future automation systems may be installed without major structural modifications.

Smart Home Ready does not imply the presence of any particular automation platform.

---

## 4.20 Future Ready

A design principle requiring that foreseeable future technologies may be integrated without unnecessary demolition or reconstruction.

---

## 4.21 Reference Model

A documented architectural model describing a recommended implementation of one or more OHS standards.

Reference models are informative.

---

## 4.22 Normative

Content that defines mandatory or optional requirements affecting conformance.

---

## 4.23 Informative

Content intended to assist understanding, provide examples, or explain rationale.

Informative content does not affect conformance.

---

## 4.24 SHALL

Indicates an absolute requirement.

---

## 4.25 SHOULD

Indicates a recommendation.

Valid reasons may exist to ignore a recommendation, but the implications shall be understood.

---

## 4.26 MAY

Indicates an optional feature or behaviour.

---

## 4.27 Interoperability

The ability of independent systems, products, or software implementations to operate together using open interfaces and documented behaviour.

---

## 4.28 Sustainability

The ability of a building to minimize environmental impact while maintaining long-term usability, durability, and economic viability throughout its service life.

---

## 4.29 Version

A uniquely identified published revision of an OHS standard.

Only published versions may be referenced for conformance.

---

## 4.30 OHS

The Open Housing Standard project and its published specifications.
