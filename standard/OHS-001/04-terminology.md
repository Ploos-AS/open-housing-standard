# 4. Terminology

## 4.1 Purpose

This section defines the terminology used throughout the Open Housing Standard (OHS).

Unless otherwise stated, these definitions apply to all OHS specifications.

Normative keywords, including **MUST**, **MUST NOT**, **REQUIRED**, **SHALL**, **SHALL NOT**, **SHOULD**, **SHOULD NOT**, **RECOMMENDED**, **MAY**, and **OPTIONAL**, shall be interpreted according to RFC 2119 and RFC 8174.

---

## 4.2 Building

A permanent residential structure intended for human habitation.

---

## 4.3 Dwelling

An individual residential unit intended for occupancy by one household.

---

## 4.4 Occupant

A person using or residing within a dwelling.

---

## 4.5 Reference House

A non-normative implementation demonstrating one possible conforming application of one or more OHS specifications.

Reference houses illustrate the standard but do not define additional requirements.

---

## 4.6 Conformance

The degree to which an implementation satisfies all applicable normative requirements of a specific OHS specification.

---

## 4.7 Implementation

A building, software system, digital model, documentation set, or other deliverable claiming conformance with one or more OHS specifications.

---

## 4.8 Specification

A published OHS document defining normative or informative requirements for a particular subject area.

---

## 4.9 Normative

Content that defines requirements affecting conformance.

---

## 4.10 Informative

Content intended solely to provide explanation, rationale, examples, or guidance.

Informative content does not affect conformance.

---

## 4.11 Vendor Neutral

A requirement that does not depend upon products or technologies from a specific manufacturer or supplier.

---

## 4.12 Open Standard

A publicly available specification that may be implemented without proprietary restrictions.

---

## 4.13 Technical Core

The designated area of a building intended to contain the primary technical infrastructure required for operation, maintenance, replacement, and future expansion.

The Technical Core provides controlled access to building services while minimizing disruption to occupied spaces.

---

## 4.14 Dry Zone

The portion of the Technical Core intended for electrical systems, communications infrastructure, control equipment, and other services requiring protection from water.

---

## 4.15 Wet Zone

The portion of the Technical Core intended for plumbing, drainage, water distribution, wastewater handling, and other services involving liquids.

---

## 4.16 Climate Zone

The portion of the Technical Core intended for ventilation, air treatment, heat recovery, heating, cooling, humidity control, and other climate-control systems.

A Climate Zone MAY be integrated into the Technical Core or implemented as a separate service area.

---

## 4.17 Service Route

A planned and documented path allowing installation, inspection, maintenance, replacement, or expansion of building services.

---

## 4.18 Accessibility

The degree to which a building can be used safely and effectively by people with varying physical abilities throughout its intended service life.

---

## 4.19 Adaptability

The ability of a building to accommodate foreseeable future changes with minimal structural intervention.

---

## 4.20 Repairability

The ability to inspect, maintain, repair, or replace individual components without unnecessary demolition or damage to unrelated building elements.

---

## 4.21 Interoperability

The ability of systems, components, software, digital models, or documentation to operate together using documented interfaces and open standards.

---

## 4.22 Lifecycle

The complete period from planning and construction through operation, maintenance, renovation, adaptation, and eventual decommissioning.

---

## 4.23 Documentation

The complete collection of drawings, specifications, digital models, configuration data, maintenance information, and other records describing a building.

---

## 4.24 Backward Compatibility

The ability of newer specifications or implementations to remain compatible with previously conforming implementations where practical.

---

## 4.25 Reference Implementation

A practical implementation demonstrating how one or more OHS specifications may be applied.

Reference implementations illustrate the standard but do not introduce additional normative requirements.

---

## 4.26 Change Log

A documented history describing changes between published versions of a specification.

---

## 4.27 Architecture Decision Record (ADR)

A permanent record documenting significant architectural or project decisions together with their rationale and consequences.

---

## 4.28 Schema

A machine-readable definition describing the permitted structure, data types, constraints, and validation rules for structured OHS data.

---

## 4.29 Reference Model

A machine-readable or human-readable model demonstrating a recommended implementation of one or more OHS specifications.

Reference models are informative unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

## 4.30 Conformance Claim

A documented statement asserting that an implementation conforms to a specific OHS specification and version.

Conformance claims shall identify the exact specification and version referenced.
