# 5. Requirements

This section defines the fundamental normative requirements applicable to all Open Housing Standard (OHS) specifications.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, all OHS standards SHALL conform to the requirements in this section.

---

## 5.1 Open Standards

OHS specifications SHALL be publicly available.

Normative requirements SHALL be documented in sufficient detail to permit independent implementation.

---

## 5.2 Vendor Neutrality

An OHS standard SHALL NOT require products or technologies from a single manufacturer.

Equivalent implementations SHALL be permitted whenever they satisfy the normative requirements.

---

## 5.3 Open Formats

Specifications SHOULD use open, documented file formats whenever practical.

Proprietary formats MAY be referenced only when no practical open alternative exists.

---

## 5.4 Long Service Life

Buildings designed according to OHS SHOULD target a minimum design service life of 100 years.

Requirements SHALL prioritize durability over short-term optimization.

---

## 5.5 Maintainability

Building components SHALL be replaceable using conventional construction methods.

Routine maintenance SHALL NOT require structural demolition.

---

## 5.6 Repairability

Critical building systems SHOULD be designed so that individual components may be repaired or replaced independently.

---

## 5.7 Accessibility

Accessibility SHALL be considered from the earliest design stages.

Requirements SHOULD enable independent use by people with varying physical abilities whenever practical.

---

## 5.8 Adaptability

Buildings SHOULD accommodate future changes in occupancy, technology, and user requirements without major structural modifications.

---

## 5.9 Technical Separation

Building services SHOULD be separated from the primary structural system.

Mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and communication systems SHOULD be independently maintainable.

---

## 5.10 Documentation

Every OHS implementation SHALL include sufficient documentation to permit inspection, maintenance, and future modification.

---

## 5.11 Interoperability

Whenever interfaces exist between systems, they SHOULD be documented and based on open standards whenever practical.

---

## 5.12 Sustainability

Sustainability SHALL be evaluated over the complete service life of the building.

Durability, repairability, and adaptability SHALL take precedence over short-term optimization.

---

## 5.13 Safety

Nothing in OHS supersedes applicable laws, regulations, or mandatory building codes.

Where conflicts exist, applicable legislation SHALL take precedence.

---

## 5.14 Extensibility

OHS standards SHOULD permit future extensions without invalidating existing conforming implementations.

---

## 5.15 Human-Centred Design

OHS standards SHALL prioritize the needs of occupants throughout the complete service life of the building.

Technical solutions SHALL support usability, comfort, safety, maintainability, and long-term value for occupants.
